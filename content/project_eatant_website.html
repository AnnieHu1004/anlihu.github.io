Eatant · Website

An intuitive and natural mobile application for booking restaurants

Project Vision

Eatant is a restaurant appointment service that primarily allows users to book restaurants within seconds. Except for a mobile application (refer to project-Eatant · Mobile), Eatant also redesigns and improves the booking process on desktop, tablet and mobile browsers, aiming to provide seamless user experiences across platforms, reach more customers with different accessibilities, and benefit 2B users.

Challenges

1) Be consistent with the mobile application in terms of core functionality and design style.
2) Search and navigate everywhere.
3) Consider how to increase retention.

Is There Any Difference for Target Users Groups?

When I was thinking about whether the target user groups have any difference between the website and mobile application, some scenarios came into my mind.

They still can cover a wide range of users group, across different ages and occupations. However, customers who use App, the ones with a higher threshold of use, are easier to develop user loyalty; while customers who use websites sometimes tend to have a one-shot deal. For example, if a tourist searches for a restaurant on Google, Eatant's page for the restaurant is shown at the top of all search results.

In another word, customers have similar needs but different consumption and usage preferences.

Competitive Audit

Before starting sketching, I did the same competitive audit on the websites of the three competitors of the mobile applications. It is quite consistent that they all have more booking steps than mobile applications. As websites for desktops and tablets have the ability to display more information, users could easily be distracted, they all show information confirmation pages. Compared to mobile applications, they also design clearer user flows, allowing users to book restaurants more naturally with less doubts in their minds.

A Simply Usability Test

After having all the digital wireframes for the desktop, I created a low-fidelity prototype and invited three participants to complete the task, choosing a restaurant from the search page and then booking it.

The good news is all participants completed the task successfully and shared almost the same click path. However, there was still some confusion for users as they tended to click the grayscale "BOOK NOW" button before they fill in all the needed information. ( The problem is participants could not tell it's unclickable until they are at the final step!)

Challenge 01 Highly Consistent with the App

Same Theme Color

All Eatant's products adopt the same theme color (#AC1B1B) to communicate a consistent brand image. The difference is website uses a less saturated red to differentiate blocks and modules as it is more suitable for large area. And it uses the theme color to highlight important information and indicate buttons.

Similar Modules on Different Pages

Both the mobile application and website have common modules, but they still have differences considering the scenarios. The most notable is the “ABOUT US” on the web page. We don't want our customers to have a one-shot deal and leave; this part can increase their loyalty.

Same High-Quality Content

No matter what platforms customers are using, Eatant always provides high-quality content- great restaurant selection, informative details, authentic and effective reviews, holding the belief in finding the best delicious for customers.

Challenge 02 Search and Navigate Everywhere

Customers may enter Eatant's website through Google keyword searching, pay-per-click advertising or banner ads. They may also enter different pages of the website, including the homepage, certain restaurant profile page or the About Us page. No matter which page-level customers are at, they can always navigate to the homepage and search page through header and footer links.

Challenge 03 Increase Retention

It is true that the website can automatically select "Sign me up to receive dining offers and news from Eatant by email." for customers when they are submiting their appointment. However, we all remember the annoyance when we open our email and find receiving countless promotion emails. In this case, Eatant provides different ways to guide customers and increase retention.
